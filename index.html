Common Dental Problems — What It Means & What To Do

Short, plain-English guidance for symptoms people search. Tap a card to learn the next step or jump to the right treatment.

Toothache / Sensitivity

Sharp or lingering pain with hot/cold often means decay, cracked tooth, or exposed dentin.

  • Do now: Rinse warm salt water; OTC pain relief if safe; avoid extremes.
  • Likely care: Filling or Root Canal, desensitizer, or crown.
Fillings Book Call

Facial Swelling / Gum Boil

May indicate spreading infection (abscess). Needs urgent evaluation.

  • Do now: Cold compress outside; do not apply heat.
  • Likely care: RCT or extraction + antibiotics if indicated.
Emergency Call

Wisdom Tooth Pain

Impacted third molars can trap food and cause gum infection (pericoronitis).

  • Do now: Warm saltwater rinses; gentle brushing.
  • Likely care: Surgical extraction + irrigation.
Extractions Book

Bleeding Gums / Bad Breath

Common in gingivitis/periodontitis. Often painless, but progressive.

  • Do now: Brush 2×/day with fluoride paste; floss/interdental brush daily.
  • Likely care: Scaling ± deep cleaning; Gum therapy.
Scaling Periodontics

Missing Tooth / Loose Denture

Bone shrinks over time; early replacement preserves bite & looks.

  • Options: Implant, Bridge, or Denture.
Implants Crowns/Bridges

Crooked / Spaced Teeth

Alignment improves hygiene, bite, and confidence.

  • Care: Braces (metal/ceramic) or clear aligners (Invisalign).
Braces Clear Aligners

Jaw Pain / Clicking (TMJ)

Often muscular or bite-related. Most cases respond to conservative care.

  • Care: Soft diet, heat packs, splints, physio; scan if persistent.
TMJ Care CBCT

Snoring / Sleep Apnea (OSA)

Custom oral appliances can reduce airway collapse in mild–moderate cases.

Sleep Dentistry

Mouth Ulcers / White Patches

Trauma, stress, or nutrition are common; persistent lesions need screening.
